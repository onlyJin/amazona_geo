Three Patterns Found Across All 72 Listings

1

Best-sellers answer "when and where" in the first bullet point.

82% of 80+ scoring listings explicitly state a usage scenario in their opening sentence: "Perfect for morning commutes, gym sessions, and hiking trips." Low-scoring listings lead with specifications.

2

Low-scoring listings describe the product. High-scoring listings describe the experience.

Compare: "Stainless steel, 32oz, BPA-free" vs "Keeps ice solid for 24 hours, fits your car cup holder, goes everywhere you go." Same product. The second one gives Rufus context to recommend it.

3

The gap isn't between "good" and "bad." It's between "complete" and "missing one dimension."

Even the worst-scoring listings had products that were fine. They just weren't telling Rufus enough about who uses them or why. It's not about being wrong — it's about being invisible to AI search.
